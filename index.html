People Also Ask - Trading Questions in India

How much money do I need to start trading in India?

Minimum: ₹500-1,000 for account opening, but recommended: ₹10,000-25,000 for meaningful learning. With smaller amounts, brokerage eats into profits. Start with amounts you can afford to lose while learning. Most successful traders recommend starting with at least ₹50,000 for proper position sizing and risk management.

Can I do intraday trading with ₹1000?

Technically yes, but not recommended. With ₹1000, after brokerage (₹20), STT, and other charges, you need 3-4% profit just to break even. Risk management becomes impossible. Better to save ₹10,000+ or practice with paper trading first. Focus on learning rather than making money with such small amounts.

Is trading a good career in India?

For most people, no. Only 10-20% of traders make consistent profits. Trading can be a career for those with: significant capital (₹10+ lakhs), proven track record, strong risk management, emotional discipline, and backup income sources. Most successful traders combine it with other income sources initially. Consider it a side activity until you prove consistent profitability.

What is the 90% rule in trading?

90% of traders lose 90% of their money in 90 days. This highlights how most beginners approach trading like gambling. The rule emphasizes the importance of education, risk management, and realistic expectations. Successful traders focus on preserving capital first, profits second. They risk only 1-2% per trade and treat trading as a business, not a get-rich-quick scheme.

Should I start with delivery or intraday trading?

Start with delivery trading. It's less stressful, no time pressure, lower brokerage (₹0 at most brokers), and good for learning market behavior. Intraday requires quick decisions, higher brokerage, and more psychological pressure. Master cash delivery first, understand risk management, then consider intraday if it suits your personality and schedule.

Can I learn trading in 1 year?

Learn basics: 3-6 months. Become profitable: 2-5 years. You can understand fundamentals quickly, but developing profitable skills takes time. Most successful traders say it took 2-3 years to become consistently profitable. Focus on education, practice with small amounts, and don't expect quick profits. Treat the first year as expensive education, not income generation.

What stocks should beginners buy in India?

Start with large-cap stocks: Reliance, TCS, HDFC Bank, Infosys, ITC, ICICI Bank, SBI, Wipro, HUL, Asian Paints. These are stable, liquid, and less volatile. Avoid penny stocks, small-caps, and volatile stocks initially. Focus on blue-chip companies with good fundamentals. Learn to analyze these before moving to riskier stocks.

Is 30 too old to start trading?

No, 30 is actually ideal. You have financial stability, emotional maturity, and better risk assessment skills than younger traders. Many successful traders start in their 30s or 40s. The key is having disposable income to learn with, realistic expectations, and treating it as a skill to develop over time, not a quick money-making scheme.

Can I start trading from home in India?

Yes, completely legal and common. You need: laptop/smartphone with internet, trading account with SEBI-registered broker, PAN card, bank account, and basic market knowledge. Most Indian traders trade from home. Ensure good internet connectivity and create a distraction-free environment for making trading decisions.

What is the safest way to trade in India?

Risk management is key: 1) Never risk more than 1-2% per trade, 2) Use stop losses religiously, 3) Start with delivery trading, 4) Avoid F&O until experienced, 5) Trade with money you can afford to lose, 6) Keep learning and maintaining trading journal, 7) Don't use borrowed money or margin initially.

How much can I earn from trading monthly in India?

Realistic expectations: Beginners should expect losses initially. Experienced traders target 2-5% monthly returns (24-60% annually). With ₹1 lakh capital, that's ₹2,000-5,000 monthly IF you're skilled. Most beginners lose money for the first 1-2 years. Focus on learning and capital preservation, not income expectations.

Do I need to pay tax on trading profits in India?

Yes, trading profits are taxable. Intraday and F&O profits: business income (up to 30% tax). Delivery gains: capital gains (15% STCG, 10% LTCG above ₹1 lakh). Keep detailed records of all trades. Consider consulting a CA for tax planning. Factor taxes into your profit calculations from the beginning.
